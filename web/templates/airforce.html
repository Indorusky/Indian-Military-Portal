AIR FORCE OPERATIONS
Major Indian Air Force operations since Independence
Kashmir Air Operations
1947 – 1948
The Indian Air Force played a decisive role during the Kashmir conflict. Dakota aircraft airlifted troops and supplies to Srinagar. This was one of the earliest strategic airlift missions in Asia. It ensured the defense of the Kashmir valley. The operation highlighted the importance of air mobility.
Indo-China War Air Operations
1962
During the 1962 conflict, the IAF focused on logistics and supply missions. Aircraft operated in high-altitude Himalayan regions. Limited offensive use was employed due to political decisions. The war exposed air defense limitations. It led to rapid modernization of the IAF.
Indo-Pak War Air Operations
1965
The 1965 war saw intense aerial combat between India and Pakistan. IAF conducted close air support and strategic bombing missions. Fighter aircraft protected ground forces. Several key enemy air bases were targeted. The conflict strengthened India’s air combat doctrine.
Indo-Pak War & Bangladesh Liberation
1971
The IAF achieved complete air superiority in the eastern sector. Precision strikes neutralized enemy airfields. Airlift and close support missions accelerated ground advances. The war concluded with a decisive Indian victory. It remains one of the IAF’s finest campaigns.
Kargil War – IAF Role (Operation Safed Sagar)
1999
The IAF provided crucial air support during the Kargil conflict. High-altitude bombing missions were conducted. Precision strikes targeted enemy positions. Aircraft operated under extreme weather conditions. The operation demonstrated air–land coordination.
Balakot Air Strike
2019
The Balakot air strike targeted terrorist training camps. It was conducted deep inside enemy territory. Precision-guided munitions were employed. The strike showcased India’s long-range strike capability. It marked a significant shift in strategic posture.
