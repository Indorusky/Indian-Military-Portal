NAVAL OPERATIONS
Major Indian Navy operations since Independence
Operation Trident
1971
Operation Trident was a decisive naval strike during the Indo-Pak War of 1971. Indian Navy missile boats attacked Karachi harbor. Several enemy vessels and fuel depots were destroyed. The operation severely crippled Pakistan’s naval capability. It marked the first successful use of anti-ship missiles in the region.
Operation Python
1971
Operation Python was a follow-up strike on Karachi harbor. It was conducted by a smaller naval task force. The attack further damaged enemy ships and oil installations. It intensified the naval blockade of Pakistan. The operation ensured Indian naval dominance in the Arabian Sea.
Operation Talwar
1999
Operation Talwar was part of India’s naval response during the Kargil conflict. The Indian Navy deployed warships to the Arabian Sea. Pakistan’s sea lines of communication were effectively threatened. This operation pressured Pakistan economically and strategically. Naval readiness played a key role in conflict escalation control.
Operation Cactus
1988
Operation Cactus was a rapid military intervention in the Maldives. Indian Navy supported the airlift of troops. The operation neutralized a coup attempt. It demonstrated India’s ability to project power in the Indian Ocean. The mission strengthened regional security cooperation.
Operation Raahat (Naval Role)
2015
Operation Raahat was a humanitarian evacuation mission from Yemen. Indian Navy ships rescued thousands of Indian and foreign nationals. Naval vessels provided medical aid and shelter. The operation showcased India’s humanitarian capability. It enhanced the Navy’s global reputation.
Anti-Piracy Operations
2008 – Present
Indian Navy has conducted continuous anti-piracy patrols in the Gulf of Aden. Numerous merchant ships have been escorted safely. Piracy threats have been neutralized through active engagement. These missions ensure maritime trade security. India remains a key contributor to global maritime safety.
