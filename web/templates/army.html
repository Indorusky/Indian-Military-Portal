ARMY OPERATIONS
Major Indian Army operations since Independence
Operation Polo
1948
Operation Polo was launched to integrate the princely state of Hyderabad into the Indian Union. The Nizam’s refusal to accede created instability. The Indian Army conducted a swift five-day operation. It prevented prolonged conflict and restored order. Hyderabad officially joined India on 17 September 1948.
Indo-Pak War (Jammu & Kashmir)
1947 – 1948
The first war between India and Pakistan erupted over Jammu and Kashmir. Tribal militias supported by Pakistan invaded the region. Indian troops were airlifted to Srinagar in a historic mission. Fierce battles were fought in mountainous terrain. The conflict resulted in the Line of Control.
Indo-China War
1962
The war was fought along the Himalayan borders with China. Indian soldiers faced extreme cold and difficult terrain. Though setbacks occurred, the war exposed critical weaknesses. It led to major military reforms and modernization. The lessons of 1962 still influence Indian defense planning.
Indo-Pak War & Liberation of Bangladesh
1971
The 1971 war was a decisive victory for India. Indian forces coordinated with Mukti Bahini fighters. Rapid advances led to the surrender of over 90,000 enemy troops. Bangladesh emerged as an independent nation. This war showcased India’s strategic excellence.
Kargil War (Operation Vijay)
1999
The Kargil conflict involved high-altitude warfare. Enemy forces occupied strategic mountain peaks. Indian soldiers carried out daring uphill assaults. Territory was reclaimed without crossing the LoC. Kargil symbolizes bravery and sacrifice. India launched Operation Vijay to recapture these strategic high-altitude peaks, resulting in fierce battles with significant casualties
Surgical Strikes
2016
Surgical strikes targeted terrorist launch pads across the border. They followed attacks on Indian military bases. Special Forces executed precise, intelligence-based missions. The operation marked a strategic policy shift. It demonstrated India’s operational capability.
